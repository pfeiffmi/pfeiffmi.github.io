A Bit About Me

I am a graduate student researcher at Oregon State University, working toward my Ph.D. in robotics.

My love for robotics started back in 2019, when a friend introduced me to my community college's robotics team. I was instantly hooked when I got to see the interdisciplinary nature of robotics, building a physical system that brings your code to life and interact with the real-world.

During the 2020 pandemic, I started to be more aware of my mental health, which sparked into an interest in the field of Psychology. This evetually lead me to discover the combination of my interests within the field of Cognitive Robotics, where a majority of my research interests reside.

My goal with my work is to develop robots that can help people in their daily lives (whether they are in critical need, just need someone to help with some chores, or to better understand ourselves) by applying the techniques in cognitive robotics. want to help develop robots that help people or help us better understand ourselves believe that robots can

When I am not in the lab, I like to watch anime, cook, bake, and make some cool parts using my 3D-printer.

2025

CuttleBot: Emulating Cuttlefish Behavior and Intelligence in a Novel Robot Design

M.A. Pfeiffer, S. Kandimalla, J. Liu, K. Hsu, E.J. Kirshner, A. Yuan, H.W. Lui, & J.L. Krichmar

A look at the first version of the iterative development of a robot that is inspired off of the morphology, neurobiology, and behavior of cuttlefish. As cuttlefish are intelligent creatures whose morphology and brains are vastly different from mammals (who are more studied), creating a robot model of cuttlefish can allow us to better understand how their brain functions and make comparisons to other animals to possibly learn something about intelligence as a whole.

In: From Animals to Animats 17, Editors: O. Brock & J.L. Krichmar, Conference: 17th International Conference on the Simulation of Adaptive Behavior (SAB 2024). Lecture Notes in Computer Science, vol 14993, pp 93–105. Springer, Cham. DOI: https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-031-71533-4_7
